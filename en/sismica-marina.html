Seismic in aquatic environments

The recognition of the materials that make up the bottoms of the seas, rivers, lakes and swamps is carried out by means of various techniques, each of them providing different types of data that help to characterize and define the geology of these funds, both in their surface and in depth. The most frequently used are:

  • BATIMETRY
  • MONOCHANAL REFLECTION SEISMIC
  • HIGH RESOLUTION REFLECTION SEISMIC
  • SIDE SWEEP SONAR
  • MAGNETOMETRY
  • METAL DETECTORS
  • TAKING OF SAMPLES THROUGH SPOONS, VIBRES, ETC.
  • PHOTOGRAPHS AND SUBMARINE VIDEOS

One of the first problems that arises in these techniques is the situation for the later representation of the acquired data in a topographic base. Therefore it is necessary and vitally important:

A navigation program: Using differential GPS (or other technique) to place at any time the position of the boat and the sensors it carries

Applications

For the study of the geological-geotechnical characteristics of the funds in: port works of all kinds, underwater emissaries, dredging, underwater installations (artificial reefs, etc.), works control, beach regeneration, underwater archeology works, biological works , Works of underwater geology, study of wetland filling, environmental studies in rivers, etc.
