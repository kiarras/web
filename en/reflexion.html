High resolution seismic reflection

The high resolution reflection seismic applied to geotechnical and hydrogeological studies is one of the most powerful geophysical tools for the study of geological formations in the first 700-1000 meters depth (adjusting the device and choosing a source of Adequate energy could be reached up to 2000 m depth).

Basically, the seismic method of reflection consists of generating a seismic wave train, using an appropriate energy source (hammer, "gun", weight drop, dynamite, etc.) and measure the travel time of said waves, once Reflected in the different layers or interfaces with sufficient acoustic impedance contrast (velocity * density), between the energy source and a series of sensors (geophones), arranged in a straight line from it (profile).

From the precise knowledge of the time employed and the velocity, the trajectories of these primary waves can be reconstructed, and the structural arrangement of the different seismic horizons can be delineated along the profile, obtaining geosmetic cuts of great definition, as shown in the The geophysical method is sensitive to changes in acoustic impedance in depth, whether positive or negative (increase or decrease, respectively, velocity with depth).

The generation / transmission of seismic waves has associated other types of unwanted waves, caused by surface conditions, random noise, multiple reflections, etc., which are recorded at the same time as the primary waves, but that today's techniques Allow for considerable attenuation by means of a multichannel record.

This technique uses various power source positions, several trace sensors and multichannel recording systems, allowing the addition of different seismic traces with a common subsurface reflection. With this recording technique field, called Common Point Subsurface or C.D.P. (Common Depth Point), a certain degree of coverage (sum of signals) is achieved, with the advantage that a great variety of geophone-energy distances are involved at each common subsurface point. In this way, by means of adequate processing, an effective attenuation of the random noise and the non-primary reflections and a marked improvement in the signal-to-noise ratio and a more reliable interpretation can be achieved by performing the "stack".

As this technique is the adaptation of oil seismic to the study of the most superficial layers of the terrain, it requires that it be carried out in the field with an exquisite acquisition methodology, in terms of the selection of acquisition and registration parameters, degree of coverage ( Minimum 2400%), small horizontal resolutions (of the order of 1.25 meters), and later in cabinet, it is necessary to carry out a process similar to the oil tanker but suitable to the treatment of the first second of record, generally.
It is essential to use powerful processing programs (oil seismic programs adapted to the short record time used in comparison with the oil records) since the variety and power of the mathematical algorithms that they use allow to obtain good final results (sections stack and migrated) .

The stack and migrated sections are to be obtained, the latter being fundamental since the interpretation must be performed on it
It is fundamental to define the velocity field in order to obtain a correct transformation of the sections time to depths and thus obtain the corresponding geosmetic cut with the definition of layer velocities. All these processes require the obtaining of static corrections of high resolution, without which the sum of signals will not be adequate.

Applications

GEOTECHNICAL: Structural studies for tunnels, dams, deep foundations, important landslides, etc. Defining the network of faults and fractures, disposition of the different existing lithologies, valuation of the Barton Q index, etc.
HYDROGEOLOGY: Structural studies for the location of aquifers, fracturing systems in impermeable formations through which water can circulate, etc.
MINING: Structural studies.
