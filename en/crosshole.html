Crosshole - seismic tomography between boreholes

With the "crosshole" test, the velocities of the primary and secondary waves (Vp, Vs) are obtained along direct paths between two points located inside a borehole at the same depth. In one of the borehole the energy source is located (emitter hole) and in the other the well geophone (receiver hole).

As in the downhole, the velocities Vp and Vs are recorded, the corresponding dynamic elastic moduli can be calculated. This method also serves to detect the existence of zones with "velocity anomalies" between boreholes.
These tests are to be carried out according to the D4428 ASTM standard, with distances between the "emitter probe" and the "receiver probe" of 3 to 10 m (one probe or several) and with the energy source (emitter) and triaxial geophone (Receiver) at the same height.

Polls (both sender and receiver) must be cased with PVC pipe and attached it firmly to the ground by an injection of slurry, which has been performed from the bottom up to avoid the presence of voids. In front of the downhole test has the drawback of requiring two mechanical probes.

The seismic tomography reconstructs an image of the internal structure of the subsoil, starting from the travel times of the seismic waves that propagate, in said medium, inside a section of ground between two surveys.
The energy source, which may be an explosive, a sparker, a pneumatic hammer, etc., is introduced into the emission survey, while the reception and surface probes record seismic waves generated; This record can be made by hydrophones and / or geophones (generally referred to as "captors"). Both the energy source and the "captors" have to be spaced on a regular basis, the situation being more favorable that the spacing of both are equal.

The methodology of work presents the following sequence:

  1. The power source is placed at a certain depth, it is triggered and the seismic waves are collected throughout the entire reception survey and on the surface between the two probes, if possible. See Figure A, where a schematic with some paths is represented
  2. The power source is placed in the next position and the operation is repeated

It is very important that the distance between probes is not excessive because the energy of the sources is limited and therefore the quality of the signal decreases as the spacing between the probes increases. Also, the method's resolving power decreases with distance, since there must always be a relationship between the size of the "anomaly" and the section of ground between the reception and emission boreholes.

The conditions for testing of seismic tomography are:

  1. The probes, of emission and reception, must be tubed in PVC and filled with water
  2. You must know the spatial position of the probes (deviation and inclination), with an accuracy of +/- 5 cm in X, Y, Z, throughout the entire survey, because with such precision should know the coordinates of the energy source and of the "captors"
  3. The probes must reach a depth that exceeds by 20-30% the depth to which the objective of the study is placed

Applications

The same as the downhole and for the study of heterogeneities between probes.
Its applications are multiple, from the location of "anomalous zones" (faults, galleries, cavities, etc.) between probes, to determine the distribution of the velocity field Seismic surveys, etc.
