Downhole

The downhole test is performed to obtain the dynamic elasticity moduli of the terrain, traversed by a survey, from the measurement of the velocity of the primary and secondary waves (Vp, Vs), along the vertical of the sounding

Elastic Modules

  • Poisson's ratio
  • Shear modulus
  • Young's Module
  • Bulk modulus

On the surface, and near the mouth of the sounding, P and S waves are generated, and these are recorded with a triaxial geophone (three components orthogonal to each other), at different depths (usually every meter) throughout the survey.
The probes must be tubed with PVC pipe and this is solidly attached to the ground by injection of grout, which must be performed from the bottom up to avoid the presence of voids.

Applications

GEOTECHNIA: For all those cases that require the knowledge of dynamic elastic moduli: foundations of dams and buildings, emboquille of tunnels, etc.
